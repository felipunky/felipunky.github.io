Parametric Design

According to wikipedia parametric design is a process based on algorithmic thinking that enables the expression of parameters and rules that, together, define, encode and clarify the relationship between design intent and design response. In other words you design through understanding the design problem in terms of what the elements that compose it mean to each other.

Image that shows a parametric model of the Ekko pavilion by Thilo Frank.

This pavilion is modeled through the understanding of the relations between the elements, in this case those elements are the "frames" that grow radially while rotating. As you can see the pavilion starts from a circle primitive, so all of the elements that compose the pavilion will be modified when the radius of such circle is changed. We could call this model paramateric as changing the radius parameter will create a new design in which the relations between elements (for example the number of frames) will remain the same.
